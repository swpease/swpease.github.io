# Noise and Cardiovascular Disease (CVD)
I mostly wanted to bring up a few ideas I thought up for another paper by an author of the above paper:

> Roscoe C, Grady ST, Hart JE, Iyer HS, Manson JE, Rexrode KM, Rimm EB, Laden F, James P. Association between Noise and Cardiovascular Disease in a Nationwide U.S. Prospective Cohort Study of Women Followed from 1988 to 2018. Environ Health Perspect. 2023 Dec;131(12):127005. doi: 10.1289/EHP12906. Epub 2023 Dec 4. PMID: 38048103; PMCID: PMC10695265. [link](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10695265/)

The authors have one heck of an adjustment set, and in spite of that, mention in their discussion that there may of course be yet more factors that affect the relationship between noise and CVD. Well, I thought of a couple:

  1. Dogs
  2. Walking

## Dogs
Dogs. Dogs both make noise and make you get exercise. In one Keigo Higashino murder mystery, when the detective first gets on the scene, an officer tells him a dog walker found the body that morning. The detective says, "Where would we be without dog walkers?". I once decided to count the proportion of dog walkers to regular walkers while going on a walk of my own, and wound up at ~50% (20ish each).

I think that dogs would constitute a... precision variable? Possibly a confounder? I guess we could stratify dog owners by Good Boys and Bad Dogs.

## Walking
Here I think is a significantly more important parameter. Maybe something like a Walkability Score could have been included; some sort of measure of physical activity beyond the BMI groupings they did include. 

I'm thinking of it as a mediator. I certainly am strongly disinclined to walk somewhere if there is no pleasant route, and traffic noise is the biggest factor for me in that respect.
